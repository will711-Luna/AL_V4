about

I spent years as an editor inside teams that were under-resourced, overloaded, and working on things that actually matter. My role was to make sure the final output respected both the subject and the reader.

Now I work independently with organizations that need more than a proofread. I help you shape the idea, structure the argument, and make decisions about what belongs on the page – and what doesn’t. The goal: documents that are accurate, readable, and honest about their limitations.

services

I usually work on one of three things: making an existing document better; planning communications for a project or program; or sitting beside your team as a quiet, long-term editorial partner.

how i work

1. Short call, clear decision. We talk for 30–45 minutes about the work, your constraints, and what has to be true for this to feel successful.

2. Lightweight plan. I send a one-page note with what I’ll do, how much I’ll touch, and how we’ll give each other feedback.

3. Deep, quiet work. You get back marked-up documents and a short written summary of decisions, questions, and options, not just a file full of tracked changes.

4. Hand-over, not drop-off. Where useful, I add a short “editor’s note” you can keep and reuse – style decisions, patterns that worked, and things to avoid next time.
